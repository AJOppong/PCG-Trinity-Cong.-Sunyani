Church History

During the latter part of 1982, some Presbyterian Church members residing at the Tonsuom Estate decided to begin morning devotions within the community. The district authorities were accordingly informed, and Mr. Kwan Watan was tasked to contact the District Director of Education for permission to use one of the classrooms at the estate’s primary school. Official approval from the District Director was received in December 1982.

Morning services commenced shortly after the New Year in 1983, following a two-week trial period. The morning devotion was officially inaugurated by Mr. Korang Boateng on 18th January 1983, with fourteen members present—four men and ten women. The founding members included Mrs. David Annor Sakyi, Twum Antwi, George Tenkorang, Korang Boateng, Florence Amankwaa, Patience Adjei Yobo, Comfort Adoma Nimpong, Ernestina Asare, Rosemary Annor Sakyi, Elizabeth Boateng, Dora Mensah, Harriet Okyere Owusu, Victoria Owusu, and Beatrice Sarpong.

On 17th February 1983, the then District Pastor, Rev. Major (Rtd.) J. Addae-Kyeremeh, visited the group and officially dedicated it. Membership grew rapidly, and by the end of 1983, the number had increased to 14 men, 13 women, and 17 children.

The morning service continued until the Sunyani Ebenezer Presbyterian Church Bible Studies and Prayer Group, led by Mr. Daniel Nyanko with approval from the Session chaired by Rev. E. Adjei Djan organized a three-day crusade from 20th to 22nd April 1989. The aim was to formally establish a church within the estate. A thanksgiving service followed on 23rd April 1989 at the estate’s primary school, attended by new converts, returning members, and active congregants from in and around the estate.

Dedicated members such as Mrs. M. B. Amwako, Osei Ba, and Kwan Boateng worked tirelessly to ensure the church became fully established. However, on 3rd September 1989, Rev. E. Adjei Djan ordered members to cease attending services at the estate due to a misunderstanding between him and the hierarchy of the preaching posts.

When Rev. Dr. Sam Prempeh assumed office as the Sunyani District Minister and Presbytery Chairperson, he and his Session charged the Evangelism Committee to replant the estate congregation. A ten-member committee—comprising Mr. E. K. Gyamfi, Korang Boateng, Edmond Fianko, J. B. Odei, Mrs. Elizabeth Boateng, Madam Elizabeth Kisiwaa, and others—was formed to oversee the re-establishment. With the District Minister’s blessing, the committee inaugurated the first full noon service on 21st February 1993. Due to the committee’s dedication, the Session unanimously agreed to formally inaugurate the young congregation on 12th December 1993. At the time of inauguration, the church had approximately 30 men, 45 women, and 45 children.

    • Inauguration and Dedication
    The official inauguration and dedication of the PCG Trinity Congregation were performed by Rev. Dr. Sam Prempeh, the then Sunyani District Minister and Brong Ahafo Presbytery Chairperson, assisted by Rev. S. Asiama Korangteng, the then Second Minister at Ebenezer Congregation. On that memorable day, 12th December 1993, the entire Ebenezer Congregation—led by the members of the Tonsuom Estates Church, accompanied by the Bible Study and Prayer Group and the brass band—marched from Sunyani to the Estates Experimental Primary School, where the dedication service was held.

    Acquisition of Land
    The piece of land on which the current church stands was acquired in 1995 through the relentless efforts of Mr. Edmond Fianko and other devoted members. Their commitment laid the foundation for the growth and stability of the PCG Trinity Congregation.

    Our Mission

    “To uphold the Centrality of the Word of God and through the enablement of the Holy Spirit, pursue a holistic ministry, so as to bring all of creation to glorify God.”

    Through:

    1. Mobilizing the entire church for prayer.
    2. Improving church growth through evangelism and nurture.
    3. Attaining self-sufficiency through effective resource mobilization.
    4. Promoting socio-economic development through advocacy and effective delivery of social service.
    5. Upholding the Reformed Tradition.
    6. Cherishing partnership with worldwide body of Christ.
