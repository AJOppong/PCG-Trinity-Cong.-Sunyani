Prayer Service | The name of the Lord is a strong tower

By Rev. Benjamin Ampofo

The Name of the Lord is a Strong Tower (Proverbs 18:10)
Read More

Full Summary: In ancient times, towers symbolized strength, safety, and refuge. Likewise, God’s name represents divine protection and deliverance for those who trust in Him. He is impenetrable in power, elevated above chaos, and accessible to the righteous who run to Him for safety (Psalm 46:1; Psalm 145:18).

Biblical Examples of Pursuers and Divine Deliverance
Pharaoh and the Israelites (Exodus 14:8–28)
Pharaoh pursued Israel to return them to slavery, but God parted the Red Sea and destroyed Egypt’s army.
→ God defends His people and defeats their pursuers.

Saul and David (1 Samuel 23:14, 25–28)
Saul’s jealousy drove him to hunt David, yet God shielded David in strongholds and frustrated Saul’s plans.
→ Trust in God ensures refuge and victory over opposition.

Jews Pursuing Paul (Acts 9:22–25; 2 Cor. 11:32–33)
Paul faced repeated plots to kill him, but God’s providence enabled his escape each time.
→ God protects His servants to fulfill His purpose.

Herod Pursuing Jesus (Matthew 2:13–16)
Herod sought to kill the infant Jesus, but God warned Joseph in a dream, leading the family safely to Egypt.
→ Divine intervention shields the righteous from harm.

Conclusion
Across Scripture, God consistently proves Himself a Strong Tower—a place of refuge, safety, and deliverance from every pursuer. Those who trust in His name find security, peace, and ultimate victory.

A strong tower withstands attacks. God’s power is unmatched, offering ultimate security against spiritual, emotional, or physical threats(Psalm46:1).

Quotations Used:Proverbs 18:10, Psalm 46:1; Psalm 145:18, 1 Samuel 23:14, 25–28, Acts 9:22–25; 2 Cor. 11:32–33, Matthew 2:13–16

Annual Harvest Service | I give to God because he loves me; I give because it benefits me

By Rev. Benjamin Ampofo

I give to God because He loves me (John 3:16)
Read More

Full Summary: The sermon teaches that giving is both an act of love and a channel of blessing. We give to God not to earn His love, but because He first loved us (John 3:16). Giving benefits the giver as well as the receiver—it opens the door for divine blessings, following the principle of sowing and reaping (Luke 6:38, 2 Cor. 9:6).

Giving expresses love: True giving comes from understanding God’s love.
Benefits of giving: It brings divine favor, strengthens faith, and builds a spiritual legacy.
God’s role: He blesses, multiplies, and protects those in covenant with Him.
Our role: Obey, give, and walk faithfully before God.
Examples: Abraham, the Widow of Zarephath, Cornelius, and the Shunammite woman all received blessings through giving.
Conclusion
Giving connects believers to God’s cycle of abundance—the hand that gives will never be empty.

Giving connects believers to God’s cycle of abundance—the hand that gives will never be empty.

Quotations Used:Luke 6:38, Malachi 3:10-12, 2Corinthians 9:6-11, John 3:16
